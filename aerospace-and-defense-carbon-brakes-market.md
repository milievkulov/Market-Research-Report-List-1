<p><p>The Aerospace and Defense Carbon Brakes market features two primary types of manufacturing processes: Chemical Vapor Infiltration (CVI) and Liquid Phase Infiltration (LPI). CVI utilizes gas-phase precursors to infuse carbon into porous materials, enhancing their strength and thermal stability, ideal for high-performance applications. In contrast, LPI involves liquid-phase materials that penetrate the substrate, promoting uniformity and density in the brake components. Both methods aim to improve braking efficiency, reduce weight, and enhance overall performance in aerospace and defense vehicles.</p></p>
